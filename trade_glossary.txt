Term: Tariff
Definition: A tax imposed by a government on goods entering or leaving the country, often used to regulate trade or generate revenue.

Term: Free Trade Agreement (FTA)
Definition: An agreement between countries to reduce or eliminate trade barriers, such as tariffs and quotas, to promote trade.

Term: Bill of Lading
Definition: A legal document issued by a carrier to a shipper, detailing the type, quantity, and destination of goods being transported.

Term: Incoterms
Definition: International commercial terms that define the responsibilities of buyers and sellers in international trade transactions.

Term: Letter of Credit
Definition: A financial document issued by a bank guaranteeing payment to a seller on behalf of a buyer, subject to specified conditions.

Term: Dumping
Definition: The practice of selling goods in a foreign market at a price below their normal value, often to gain market share.

Term: Countervailing Duties
Definition: Additional tariffs imposed to offset subsidies provided by a foreign government to its exporters.

Term: Customs Valuation
Definition: The process of determining the value of imported goods for the purpose of assessing customs duties.

Term: Non-Tariff Barriers
Definition: Restrictions on trade other than tariffs, such as quotas, embargoes, or technical standards.

Term: Harmonized System (HS) Code
Definition: An internationally standardized system of names and numbers to classify traded products for customs purposes.
